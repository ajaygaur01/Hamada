Project Overview 
This is the design brief for the B2B wholesale website, built under Hamada Global Trading Pvt Ltd 
— the India arm of Hamada Tea Co. Ltd, a Japanese tea company founded in 1975. 
The website targets café owners, yoga studios, hotels, wellness brands, bakeries, and specialty 
retailers who want to source premium Japanese tea in bulk. 
The existing Hamada Global Trading website (hamadaglobaltrading.com) serves as the product 
and content reference and the new B2B site inherits its product catalogue and brand story but is 
redesigned for a smoother, conversion-focused wholesale experience. 
The design stays consistent with the existing visual identity. 
The website must do two things well: make it effortless for a new buyer to order a sample with 
zero friction, and make it seamless for a verified business buyer to place and repeat bulk orders. 
 Every design decision should serve this sample-to-bulk conversion journey.
Brand Aesthetic 
This brand is all about quiet luxury not loud e-commerce. 
The site should feel like walking into a well-curated Japanese tea room, not browsing a 
marketplace. 
The mood is calm, precise, and trustworthy. 
The reference brand for aesthetic direction is Kettl (kettl.co) — a Japanese matcha brand that 
nails premium wholesale positioning without feeling cold or corporate.
The site must feel unmistakably Japanese in its restraint and attention to detail, while remaining 
warm enough for an Indian business audience.
Colour Palette 
Forest green #4C632E is the primary brand colour and should anchor the site headers, CTAs, and 
nav. 
Sage green #9AA958 for accents and secondary elements.
Warm brown #635233 for earthing tones.
Pale gold and cream #E7DDC1 as the primary background and never clinical white. Deep green 
#3E4F25 for depth. 
The site should feel warm and organic, not sterile.
Typography 
Yeseva One for headings and brand moments. 
Muli Light or Regular for all body copy, product descriptions, and UI text.
Didot Italic for subheadings and accent text where needed. Noto Sans Japanese for any 
Japanese characters used on the site. 
Maximum two fonts active on any given page. No pure black, instead use dark green or brown for 
headings. 
Generous line height and letter spacing throughout to convey the brand’s Japanese sensibility 
shows up in how text breathes on a page.
Full Product Catalogue to List on the Website 
The website must carry all products currently listed on hamadaglobaltrading.com across two 
categories.
The first category is Premium Japanese Teas. 
Matcha is available in four grades — ceremonial, premium, culinary, and ingredient and comes in 
30g, 100g, 500g, and 1kg foil pack or zip lock formats. 
Storage requires refrigeration at 10–12°C, resealing within 30–40 minutes of opening, and shelf life 
is 18 months. 
Sencha is a deep-steamed Japanese green tea with a smooth, less grassy profile suited for 
cafés, iced teas, and daily retail blends. 
Available in 100g, 500g, and 1kg. Shelf life 24 months. 
Hojicha is a roasted green tea with a warm, nutty aroma and minimal caffeine, popular for bakery 
products, ice creams, and cold brews. Available in 100g, 500g, and 1kg with shelf life 24 months. 
Hojicha Powder is the powdered version of hojicha and has the same roasted character, suited 
for the same bakery and ice cream use cases but in powder format.
Available in 100g, 500g, and 1kg. Shelf life 24 months. 
Genmaicha is a blend of sencha and roasted brown rice that is mellow and toasty and suited for 
entry-level tea menus and meal accompaniment. 
Available in 100g, 500g, and 1kg. Shelf life 24 months.
The second category is Instant Teas and Ready Formats. 
Matcha Latte Premix is a sweetened blend of premium matcha, milk powder, and jaggery and 
can be prepared by just adding hot water or milk, no whisk required.
Suited for cafés, vending machines, airline service, and retail packs.
Hojicha Latte Premix is the same format as hojicha powder, which is warm, nutty, no whisk 
required. Same use cases as the matcha premix. 
Sencha Green Tea Bags use nylon pyramid bags with no staples or microplastics, made from 
deep-steamed Fukamushi sencha. 
Formats are 3g x 25 bags and 100 bag packs.
 Suited for hotel rooms, wellness kits, and retail gifting. 
Hojicha Tea Bags are whole-leaf roasted green tea in pre-portioned sachets, gentle on the 
stomach and rich in aroma. Formats are 2g x 25 bags and 100 bag packs. Suited for after-meal 
offerings, cafés, and home brewing.
Each product page should show the product name, a one-line descriptor, use case 
suggestions, available formats and quantities, storage instructions, and shelf life. 
Two purchase options, sample and bulk Tobe shown as distinct sections.
Homepage 
The homepage has one job: tell a business buyer they are in the right place and give them an 
immediate next step. 
The hero section must show two distinct CTAs side by side :Order a Sample and Place a Bulk 
Order. 
Order a Sample is the primary CTA for first-time visitors that is softer and inviting. Place a Bulk 
Order is secondary, for returning or ready buyers. 
Below the hero: a brief brand story (Hamada Tea Co., founded in Japan in 1975, now serving 
India’s premium tea market), a featured products section showing three to four hero SKUs, social 
proof in the form of client café or hotel logos if available, and a trust block covering sourcing 
provenance from Kagoshima, quality certifications, and delivery reliability. 
The existing site mentions awards and certifications and these should be prominently featured in 
the trust section.
Products and Shop Page 
All products are visible to everyone across two clear sections: Premium Japanese Teas and 
Instant Teas and Ready Formats.
 Sample sizes and sample pricing are shown openly to all visitors.
 Bulk pricing per 100g, 500g, 1kg is either shown with a soft lock UI or revealed only after login  to 
be confirmed with Hema. 
Each product card shows product name, tea type, key use case, and sample availability at a 
glance. 
Filtering should allow visitors to browse by tea type and by use case such as café menu, bakery, 
hotel amenity, or retail.
Single Product Page 
Product name and grade or type as the headline. 
Full description covering origin, tasting profile, and use case recommendations. Storage 
instructions and shelf life are clearly stated. 
Two purchase options presented as distinct sections: the sample option open to all with no login, 
and the bulk order option with login and first-time GST verification required. A brief brewing or 
preparation guide below. 
Photography should be full-width and natural — no white box cutouts, no floating product 
images.
How It Works Page 
A simple three-step visual explainer for first-time B2B visitors. 
Step one: Order a sample no account needed, delivered to your door. 
Step two: Test with your team and menu. 
Step three: Return to place your verified bulk order. Three line-work icons in brand green, three 
short paragraphs, one CTA at the bottom pointing back to the product catalogue.
Wholesale and B2B Landing Page 
Dedicated page for serious business buyers explaining minimum order quantities, pricing tiers, 
available formats, lead times, and packaging options including bulk packaging and private label. 
A brief section on ideal wholesale customer types  cafés, bakeries, hotels, airlines, wellness 
studios, and gourmet retailers. 
End with a dual CTA: Order a Sample to get started, or Contact Us for custom or large-volume 
enquiries.
Sample Order Flow 
No account creation required.  
Fields: name, business name, email, phone, delivery city or pin code, and UPI transaction ID for 
payment confirmation. 
A note at the top of the form: “Please complete payment to [UPI ID] before submitting. Your 
sample will be dispatched once payment is verified.” 
Payment via UPI or Razorpay. Automated confirmation email with order summary and expected 
dispatch timeline after submission.
Bulk Order Flow 
Login required. 
On first bulk order, the buyer completes a one-time GST verification. 
The GST input field accepts the 15-digit GSTIN format with inline validation. 
On successful verification via API, the system fetches the registered business name for the buyer 
to confirm. 
The account is permanently marked as Verified Wholesale Buyer and a subtle badge visible in the 
account dashboard. 
From the second bulk order onwards, the buyer sees saved address, saved cart, and a reorder 
shortcut from order history.
Account Dashboard 
For verified wholesale buyers.
 Shows: verification status badge, order history with samples and bulk orders listed separately, 
reorder button on each past order, downloadable GST-compliant invoices, and saved shipping 
addresses.
Admin Panel for Hema’s Team 
View all orders in one place, mark orders as dispatched with tracking links, see customer 
verification status, export order data as CSV, and manage product listings and pricing without 
developer involvement.
Navigation Structure 
Primary nav: Products, How It Works, About, Wholesale, Contact. Secondary nav top right: Login 
and a persistent Order a Sample button visible on every page.
About Page 
Brand story of Hamada Tea Co. — founded 1975 in Japan, 40 years of tea craftsmanship, now 
bringing that legacy to India through Hamada Global Trading Pvt Ltd. 
Hema’s personal connection to the brand and the Kagoshima sourcing story. Keep it personal and 
grounded. 
One or two strong photographs. 
Minimal text, maximum feeling.
Contact Page 
WhatsApp link as the primary contact method. Email: sales@chirantea.in. 
Phone: 9899923445. 
A short inquiry form for large or custom orders with fields for name, business name, email, 
message, and optional monthly requirement estimate.
Mobile Responsiveness 
The site must be fully responsive. 
Many café and studio managers will visit on mobile, especially arriving from Instagram. The 
sample order flow must be flawless on mobile and minimal scrolling, clear CTAs, and fast loading.
What to Avoid 
No cluttered product grids. 
No aggressive promotional banners or popups. 
No generic e-commerce templates. 
No pure black in the UI. 
No more than two fonts active at once. 
No floating cut-out product images.
 No loud or stereotypical Japanese motifs used decoratively. 
No clinical white backgrounds.
